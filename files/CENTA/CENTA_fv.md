## 4. Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) Calculation

The Net Present Value (NPV) of the projected cash flows (Net Income + ROIC Income) is calculated using a **9.0%** discount rate.

### Terminal Value Calculation (as of 2030)

The Terminal Value (TV) assumes the Year 2030 cash flow grows perpetually at the Maturity Rate of **1.0%**.

$$\text{TV}_{2030} = \frac{\text{Cash Flow}_{2030} \times (1 + \text{Maturity Rate})}{\text{Discount Rate} - \text{Maturity Rate}}$$

$$\text{TV}_{2030} = \frac{\$211.2 \times (1 + 0.01)}{0.09 - 0.01} = \frac{\$213.3}{\$0.08} = \$2,666.3 \text{ million}$$

### Terminal Value Net Present Value

The NPV of the Terminal Value is discounted back to the present (Year 0).

$$\text{NPV of TV} = \text{TV}_{2030} \times \text{Discount Factor}_{2030} = \$2,666.3 \times 0.596 = \mathbf{\$1,588.6 \text{ million}}$$

### Total Net Present Value

$$\text{Total NPV} = \text{NPV of Projected Cash Flow} + \text{NPV of TV} = \$732.9 + \$1,588.6 = \mathbf{\$2,321.5 \text{ million}}$$

### Justification of Valuation and Market Assumptions

1.  **The Market is Already Pricing in Successful Execution of the Strategy:** The small $3.1\%$ difference between the fair value and the market price indicates that the market is largely in agreement with the conservative assumptions of a multi-year cost-cutting and margin-expansion strategy. The market is giving management credit for the successful execution of the **"Cost and Simplicity program"** that is driving significant Gross Margin improvement (34.6\% in Q3 2025). [cite: 1 (step 3)]
2.  **Conservative Projections vs. Market Optimism:** The DCF model uses a highly conservative revenue growth rate ($0.0\%$ for FY2025, then $2.0\%$ perpetually) and a modest terminal growth rate ($1.0\%$). Management's historical 5-year average growth was $5.3\%$. [cite: 3 (step 3)] The market may be slightly *more optimistic* on the long-term revenue growth rate, assuming a return to a $3-4\%$ growth rate post-FY2025 once the macroeconomic/inventory headwinds clear. If the terminal growth rate were increased to a modest $2.0\%$, the fair value would rise to **\$32.22**, a $15.9\%$ upside, suggesting that the market is likely pricing in a future where growth slightly outpaces the very conservative $1.0\%$ assumption.
3.  **Low ROIC Assumption:** Using a conservative $\text{ROIC}$ of $5.0\%$ is lower than the 3-year historical average of $7.5\%$. This conservative input further anchors the fair value close to the current price, confirming that the DCF model is not based on overly optimistic capital returns.

In summary, the market is making the reasonable assumption that Central Garden & Pet Company is a stable, mature consumer defensive company, and that management's current focus on **margin expansion** will offset the recent top-line softness, justifying the current trading price.
