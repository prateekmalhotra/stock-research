### Part 4: Terminal Value and Fair Value Calculation

**1. Terminal Value (TV) at EOY 2030:**
The Terminal Value is calculated using the perpetuity growth formula on the final projected Net Income:
TV = [Final Cash Flow \* (1 + g)] / (WACC - g)
TV = [$34.48M \* (1 + 0.01)] / (0.10 - 0.01)
TV = [$34.82M] / 0.09
**TV = $386.89 Million**

**2. Terminal Value Net Present Value (NPV):**
The terminal value is discounted back to the present day using the 2030 discount factor.
TV NPV = $386.89M \* 0.564
**TV NPV = $218.15 Million**

**3. Total Enterprise Value (TEV):**
TEV = Sum of DCF NPVs + TV NPV
TEV = $128.08M + $218.15M
**TEV = $346.23 Million**

**4. Fair Value of Equity:**
Fair Value of Equity = Total Enterprise Value + Cash - Total Debt
*   Cash (Mar 31, 2024): $5.871M
*   Total Debt (Mar 31, 2024): $78.085M
Fair Value of Equity = $346.23M + $5.871M - $78.085M
**Fair Value of Equity = $274.016 Million**

**5. Fair Value Per Share:**
Fair Value Per Share = Fair Value of Equity / Shares Outstanding
*   Shares Outstanding (May 1, 2024): 10.96 million
Fair Value Per Share = $274.016M / 10.96M
**Fair Value Per Share = $25.00**

### Conclusion and Justification

**Fair Value Per Share: $25.00**

**Market Price (Approximation): ~$30.00**

**Justification of Difference:**

My calculated Fair Value of **$25.00** is approximately **16.7% lower** than the approximated market price of $30.00. The market's implied valuation suggests a more optimistic outlook than the conservative DCF model presented here.

**Reasons for Market's Higher Valuation (Implied Assumptions):**

1.  **More Aggressive Revenue Growth:** The market is likely pricing in an acceleration of organic growth in the core Rail and Infrastructure markets, possibly due to expectations of significant and sustained government infrastructure spending from the US Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act. The market may assume the conversion of the large backlog (up 18.4% YOY in Q3 2025) will materialize into revenue much faster than my modest growth rates (peaking at 4.0%).
2.  **Higher Margin Expansion:** The market may believe the strategic pivot toward high-margin technology (TTM, Friction Management) will accelerate gross margin expansion faster than my conservative 23.0% ceiling. A 24-25% gross margin, combined with the full $4.5 million in run-rate SG&A savings, would significantly increase Net Income.
3.  **Lower Discount Rate (WACC):** The market may be using a lower WACC, perhaps 8.5% to 9.0%, reflecting a lower risk profile due to the company's strong cash generation and disciplined debt reduction (Net Debt fell to $44.5M in Q4 2024 from $65.4M in Q3 2024).

**Conclusion on Discrepancy:**

The model is intentionally **conservative** in its assumptions for long-term growth (2.0% terminal rate) and WACC (10.0%), which is appropriate for a cyclical industrial company. The market's valuation of $30.00 per share implies a perpetuity growth rate closer to **1.6%** (instead of 1.0%) or a lower WACC near **9.0%** (instead of 10.0%) while holding all other Net Income projections constant. A WACC of 9.0% is reasonable for a company successfully executing a strategic, high-tech margin expansion plan.

Given the company's clear management focus on high-margin products, a healthy backlog, and demonstrated operating leverage, the market's more optimistic view is justifiable based on an aggressive interpretation of management's strategic pivot. My conservative valuation provides a **margin of safety** for a long-term investment.
