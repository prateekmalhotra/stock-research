### IV. Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) Analysis

**A. Discount Rate (WACC Proxy):**

*   **Assumption:** A **Discount Rate of 9.0%** will be used. This is a reasonable and conservative estimate for a mature, publicly traded company like Aramark, which has stable but low-margin business, a moderate risk profile, and significant debt (WACC is typically higher than a low-risk rate).

**B. Terminal Value (Maturity Rate):**

*   **Assumption:** A conservative, long-term **Maturity (Perpetual Growth) Rate of 2.0%** will be used. This assumes the company will grow slightly faster than long-term inflation and U.S. GDP, reflecting its ability to consistently win new business and increase pricing.

**C. Net Present Value (NPV) Calculation (using 2030 Net Income as Proxy for Free Cash Flow):**

$$Terminal Value = \frac{\text{Cash Flow}_{\text{2030}} \times (1 + \text{Maturity Rate})}{\text{Discount Rate} - \text{Maturity Rate}}$$

$$Terminal Value = \frac{\$623.00 \times (1 + 0.02)}{0.09 - 0.02} = \frac{\$635.46}{0.07} = \$9,078.00 \text{ million}$$

### VI. Conclusion and Justification

The calculated **Fair Value Per Share** for Aramark (ARMK) is **$7.64**.

As of the current date (assumed to be February 17, 2026, based on the recent Q1 2026 filing), the actual market price of ARMK stock is likely to be significantly higher than this valuation.

**Justification for the Discrepancy (Market vs. Calculated Value):**

The market's valuation of ARMK is likely based on significantly more aggressive growth and margin assumptions, particularly in the Terminal Value calculation, which accounts for the vast majority of the calculated Enterprise Value.

1.  **Lower Discount Rate (WACC):** The market may be using a lower cost of capital (Discount Rate) than the conservative 9.0% assumption. A lower WACC significantly increases the NPV. *For example, an 8.0% Discount Rate with a 2.0% Maturity Rate would increase the Terminal Value by over 40%*.
2.  **Higher Terminal Growth Rate:** A small increase in the Perpetual Growth Rate has a massive impact. The market may be expecting long-term growth closer to 3.0% or 3.5%, reflecting the company's strong net new business pipeline and ability to consistently secure new contracts (even above organic growth).
3.  **Higher Margin Trajectory:** The market is likely pricing in a faster and higher realization of the guided AOI margin expansion. The management's focus on cost discipline and supply chain efficiencies suggests a runway for substantial profitability improvement. My conservative assumption of only 15 basis points of Net Income Margin expansion may be far too low compared to market expectations, especially as the high-margin Uniform Services business has been spun off, leaving the core FSS business poised for potential margin scale.
4.  **Deleveraging Focus:** Management has a stated goal to reduce the leverage ratio below 3.0x by the end of fiscal 2026. Successful deleveraging would reduce future interest expense, directly increasing Net Income (my cash flow proxy) and reducing the total debt in the final calculation, both of which would raise the Fair Value.

**Conclusion:** The conservative DCF model suggests that the market is valuing Aramark not just on its immediate guidance but on a strong conviction that the company's margin expansion initiatives will continue long past 2030, and that its robust revenue growth engine (Net New Business) is structurally durable and will continue to grow at a high-single-digit pace for a longer period of time, warranting a lower risk/discount rate and a higher long-term growth rate.
