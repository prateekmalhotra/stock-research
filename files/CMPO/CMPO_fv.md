### **3. Net Income and Cash Flow Projections**

Management has demonstrated strong margin performance, with a non-GAAP Gross Margin of **59%** in Q3 2025, a significant improvement from the prior year.

The **Pro Forma Adjusted EBITDA** guidance provides a good proxy for operating profitability.

*   **2026 Implied EBITDA Margin:** \$190M (Adj. EBITDA Guidance) / \$510M (Net Sales Guidance) = **37.25%**.

To get from Adjusted EBITDA to Net Income (our proxy for free cash flow to equity), we must estimate Depreciation & Amortization (D&A), Interest Expense, and Taxes.

*   **D&A and SG&A:** I will assume D&A is included in the operating expenses, and use a conservative Net Income Margin derived from the 2026 guidance.
*   **Interest Expense:** Based on the \$190M total debt (principal) and a reasonable interest rate of 8%, the annual interest expense is approximately \$15.2 million.
*   **Tax Rate:** A conservative corporate tax rate of 25% will be applied, even though the company may have deferred tax assets.

#### **Rates Used**

| Rate | Value | Justification |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Discount Rate (WACC Proxy)** | **12.0%** | A conservative rate for a small-cap, high-growth-potential company with operational execution risk and a complex accounting/share structure. |
| **Terminal Growth Rate (Maturity Rate)** | **2.0%** | A very conservative rate, slightly above long-term inflation, assuming the company reaches maturity and maintains a modest growth rate in line with or slightly below global GDP. |

#### **Net Present Value (NPV) Calculation**

**Terminal Value (TV) in 2030:**

TV = (2030 Cash Flow * (1 + Terminal Growth Rate)) / (Discount Rate - Terminal Growth Rate)
TV = (\$170.9M * 1.02) / (0.12 - 0.02)
TV = \$174.3M / 0.10

**Market Price Justification (Assuming Market Price ~\$10.00 - \$11.00)**

My calculated fair value of **\$12.37** is **higher** than the assumed current market price of **\$10.00 - \$11.00**.

**Reasons for the Difference (Justifying a Higher Fair Value):**

The market's current valuation appears to be applying a higher discount rate or a more conservative growth profile than the one I used, likely due to a few key factors:

1.  **Accounting Complexity and Spin-Off Risk:** The recent structural shift and the reliance on non-GAAP figures introduce complexity and perceived risk for investors. The market may be discounting the stock due to the difficulty in analyzing the GAAP financials, which show significant non-cash losses from warrant and earnout liabilities. My model *explicitly* addresses this by using the non-GAAP figures that reflect the underlying business economics, which I believe is the correct fundamental approach.
2.  **Husky Acquisition Integration Risk:** The announcement of the Husky Technologies acquisition is a significant development. The market may be factoring in substantial execution and integration risk, potentially hindering future growth and margin expansion, which would justify a lower share price. My model assumes successful integration and realization of the synergistic growth potential over time.
3.  **Arculus (Fintech) Uncertainty:** While Arculus achieved a positive net contribution in Q4 2024, its overall contribution and long-term success remain uncertain, which could be another source of market conservatism.

**My Assumptions Justified:**

*   **Revenue Engine:** The core assumption of continued, albeit slowing, high growth (10% to 4.7%) is highly justified by the **<1% penetration** of the metal card market and the strategic addition of Husky. The market opportunity is significant, and my growth rates are conservative given the low penetration starting point.
*   **Margins:** The **25.0% Net Income Margin** is conservative, as it is based on a conservative translation from the strong 37.25% EBITDA margin and a high 59% Q3 Gross Margin. If the company maintains or improves margins, the fair value would be significantly higher.
*   **Discount Rate:** The **12.0% Discount Rate** is appropriately conservative for a company facing the aforementioned risks, suggesting that a successful execution of the management's strategy should yield a higher valuation than the current market price.

**Conclusion:** The DCF suggests the stock is **undervalued** relative to the company's stated growth opportunity, robust non-GAAP margins, and clear management guidance for 2025/2026. The market is likely overly penalizing the stock for the temporary complexities and execution risk associated with the accounting changes and the new acquisition.
