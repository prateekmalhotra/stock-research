### **VI. Conclusion and Justification**

**Fair Value per Share (VOYA):** **\$61.22**

The calculated fair value of **\$61.22** is **16.8% lower** than the current market price of **\$73.55**.

#### **Justification for the Discrepancy**

The market is making a more optimistic set of assumptions about Voya Financial's future than the conservative model presented here.

**Market's Optimistic Assumptions (Why the stock trades higher):**

1.  **Higher Growth/AUM & Margin Expansion:** The market is likely placing a significantly higher value on Voya's fee-based businesses (Wealth Solutions and Investment Management) due to the recent strong net inflows and margin expansion guidance. The model's 6.0% Net Income Margin is highly conservative given the TTM Adjusted Operating Margin of 28.1\%. The market may be assuming:
    *   **Higher Revenue Growth:** Sustained growth closer to the 7-8\% TTM rate, driven by the 'flywheel' effect of strong market performance and successful asset gathering (the \$30B Q1 2025 inflows for Wealth are significant).
    *   **Higher Long-Term Net Income Margin:** A more aggressive Net Income Margin, perhaps closer to **8.0% - 10.0%**, which is more in line with a 'capital-light' business model and management's focus on expense discipline. If the Net Income Margin were increased to just 7.0\% across the board, the Fair Value would increase to **\$73.34**, nearly aligning with the current market price.

2.  **Lower Discount Rate:** The market may perceive Voya's cash flows as less risky than the **9.0%** WACC used, given its capital-light shift and focus on recurring fee-based revenue. A lower WACC (e.g., 8.0%) would significantly increase the Net Present Value of future cash flows.

3.  **Aggressive Terminal Growth:** The market may assume the company can maintain a higher growth rate for longer, or a higher Terminal Growth Rate (**3.0%** or more) in perpetuity, reflecting a belief in Voya's ability to compound AUM/AUA effectively.

**Conservative Model's Justification (Why the calculated value is lower):**

The model is intentionally conservative, as per the rules:

1.  **Conservative Revenue Growth:** The model's growth assumption tapers quickly (from 7.0\% to 3.0\%), accounting for the cyclical nature of asset management and the difficulty of continually landing large, one-time recordkeeping wins like the \$30B in Q1 2025.
2.  **Conservative Net Income Margin:** The **6.0%** Net Income Margin is used to conservatively buffer against potential adverse claims experience in the Health segment (e.g., the Stop Loss issue mentioned by management), and the inherent volatility in investment income/net gains/losses that often causes a large gap between Adjusted Operating Earnings and GAAP Net Income.
3.  **High Discount Rate:** The **9.0%** WACC provides a necessary margin of safety against potential macroeconomic risks and competition in the crowded wealth and asset management space.

In summary, the market appears to be valuing VOYA based on the successful execution of its "capital-light" strategy, believing that the resulting margins and net flow momentum will persist at a higher rate than the conservative 6.0% Net Income Margin and rapidly-tapering revenue growth assumed in this DCF model. **The primary driver of the value gap is likely the difference in the projected long-term Net Income Margin and/or the Discount Rate assumed.**
