*   The incremental cash from ROIC is calculated as: **Previous Year's Adjusted Cash Flow * ROIC** (6.5%).

## 5. Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) Model

### Assumptions:
1.  **Discount Rate (WACC/Cost of Equity):** A conservative but reasonable rate of **10.0%** is used, reflecting the volatility and inherent retail risk of the business.
2.  **Terminal Growth Rate:** A very conservative long-term maturity rate of **1.0%** is used, which is below the long-term US inflation target, reflecting VSCO's slow-growth/mature-industry status in North America.

### DCF Calculation:

The Net Present Value (NPV) is calculated using the Adjusted Cash Flow and the Discount Rate (10.0%).

| Year | Adjusted Cash Flow (Millions USD) | Discount Factor (1 / (1 + 0.10)^n) | Present Value (PV) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **FY 2025** (n=1) | $174 | 0.909 | $158 |
| **FY 2026** (n=2) | $200 | 0.826 | $165 |
| **FY 2027** (n=3) | $215 | 0.751 | $161 |
| **FY 2028** (n=4) | $230 | 0.683 | $157 |
| **FY 2029** (n=5) | $245 | 0.621 | $152 |
| **FY 2030** (n=6) | $257 | 0.564 | $145 |
| **Sum of PV of Explicit Period** | | | **$938** |

### Terminal Value (TV) Calculation:

The Terminal Value is calculated using the perpetuity growth method at the end of FY 2030:
TV = [FY 2030 Adjusted Cash Flow * (1 + Terminal Growth Rate)] / (Discount Rate - Terminal Growth Rate)
TV = [$257 * (1 + 0.01)] / (0.10 - 0.01)
TV = $260 / 0.09 = **$2,889 million**

### Terminal Value Present Value:

PV of TV = Terminal Value * FY 2030 Discount Factor
PV of TV = $2,889 * 0.564 = **$1,629 million**

### Total Equity Value:

Total Enterprise Value (TEV) = Sum of PV of Explicit Period + PV of Terminal Value
TEV = $938 + $1,629 = **$2,567 million**

Net Equity Value = TEV + Total Cash & Cash Equivalents - Total Debt
Net Equity Value = $2,567 + $249 - $1,351
Net Equity Value = **$1,465 million**

**Justification for the Difference:**

Our model's fair value is very close to the market price, suggesting the market is making similar, conservative assumptions.

*   **Market Assumption:** The market is likely pricing VSCO as a low-to-mid single-digit growth retail company (2.0% - 3.5% revenue growth) that is executing a difficult turnaround, stabilizing North America while relying on higher-growth international channels. The premium (Market Price of $20.00 vs. Fair Value of $18.31) suggests the market might be:
    *   **Projecting a slightly higher ROIC:** The market may believe the company can sustain its 3-year average ROIC of **7.58%** or higher, as the recent operational improvements take hold. If the ROIC were 7.58% instead of 6.5%, the fair value would increase.
    *   **Expecting faster International Growth:** The market might be assigning a higher probability to the International segment (20%+ recent growth) maintaining a strong double-digit growth rate for longer, which would increase the explicit forecast revenue and net income.
    *   **Using a lower Discount Rate:** The market may perceive a slightly lower risk (WACC) than the conservative 10.0% used in this model, perhaps closer to 9.0%-9.5%, which would increase the present value of all cash flows.

**Conclusion:**

The valuation suggests that VSCO is fairly valued by the market, which appears to be taking a conservative view on its long-term growth and profitability, aligning closely with the guarded assumptions based on management's turnaround strategy. The small difference is likely attributable to the market's slightly more optimistic view on the company's ability to capitalize on its high-growth international segment and maintain better-than-average Return on Invested Capital.
