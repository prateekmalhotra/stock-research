### Justification of Discrepancy

The calculated fair value of **$15.28** is significantly lower than the current market price of **$27.44**. This implies that the market is making a much more optimistic set of assumptions about Fluidra's future growth and profitability than the conservative model presented.

**Market Assumptions vs. Model Assumptions:**

1.  **Revenue Growth:**
    *   **Model:** Assumes a conservative growth profile, peaking at 5.0% in 2026 (post-simplification bump) and quickly normalizing to a conservative structural growth of 3.0-4.0% for the mature aftermarket business.
    *   **Market:** The market is likely pricing in a **higher, sustained growth rate (e.g., 6-8%+ per year)** for a longer period, perhaps anticipating a quicker or stronger recovery in the new build segment or more aggressive market share gains in faster-growing regions like North America (Sun Belt). The market may also be projecting a more material impact from their strategic digital and M&A investments (like Aiper and Pooltrackr) than the conservative model.

2.  **Profitability and Margin Improvement:**
    *   **Model:** Assumes a Net Income Margin that peaks at a conservative **12.0%** and remains stable.
    *   **Market:** The market is likely anticipating the full €100M in Simplification Program savings to translate into a much higher Net Income Margin, perhaps reaching **15% or more** and sustaining that due to operational efficiency and pricing power. Furthermore, the model uses Net Income, which includes non-cash Purchase Price Amortization (PPA) which management repeatedly mentions as an adjustment. The market is likely pricing off **Adjusted Net Profit** or **Cash Net Profit**, which are significantly higher (€1.365 Adjusted EPS for 2025, which would imply an Adjusted Net Profit of ~€260M and a higher margin than the 11.9% Net Income margin used). If the market's assumed cash flow is based on the higher *Adjusted* Net Profit, the NPV would be higher.

3.  **Discount and Terminal Rate:**
    *   **Model:** Uses a conservative **10.0%** Discount Rate and a low **2.0%** Terminal Growth Rate.
    *   **Market:** The market is likely using a **lower Discount Rate (WACC)**, reflecting a lower perceived systematic risk or better long-term financing costs, and a **higher Terminal Growth Rate (e.g., 3.0% or 3.5%)**, believing Fluidra's market leadership and aftermarket focus justify a stronger perpetual growth assumption.

**Conclusion:**

The calculated fair value is a **highly conservative estimate** based on translating all management's positive commentary into very cautious numerical assumptions (conservative long-term growth, margin cap, high discount rate). The market's current valuation of $27.44 suggests investors believe Fluidra will:
1.  Sustain an annual revenue growth rate well above 4% for the entire projection period.
2.  Translate its cost savings and aftermarket strength into a significantly higher Net Income Margin (closer to 15-18%).
3.  Continue to de-lever and improve its WACC.

To match the market price of $27.44, the valuation would require a sustained average Net Income Margin of approximately **17%** from 2026 onwards, or a combination of a lower WACC (e.g., 8.0%) and a higher perpetual growth rate (e.g., 3.5%). The conservative model suggests the stock is currently **overvalued** based on a prudent assessment of future financial performance for a company operating in a mature industry.
