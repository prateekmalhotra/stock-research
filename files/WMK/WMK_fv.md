## 4. Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) Analysis

### DCF Assumptions

*   **Discount Rate (Conservative but Reasonable):** I will use a **Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC)** proxy of **8.0%**. This is a conservative WACC for a low-risk, mature, geographically-focused retailer with a strong balance sheet (low debt, high cash).
*   **Maturity Rate (Terminal Growth Rate) (Very Conservative):** A very conservative rate of **1.0%** is used. This is well below the long-term US GDP growth rate, reflecting the highly competitive and mature grocery industry.

### Net Present Value (NPV) Calculation

The Net Income is treated as the projected Free Cash Flow to Equity (FCFE) for the DCF calculation, as per the rule: "Assume that net income for each year goes straight into cash for the next year."

| Year (n) | Projected Net Income (FCFE) | Discount Factor (8.0%) | **NPV of Cash Flow** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **2025** | \$107.89 | 0.9259 | \$99.81 |
| **2026** | \$110.93 | 0.8573 | \$95.11 |
| **2027** | \$113.81 | 0.7938 | \$90.35 |
| **2028** | \$116.55 | 0.7350 | \$85.67 |
| **2029** | \$119.12 | 0.6806 | \$81.07 |
| **2030** | \$121.51 | 0.6302 | \$76.58 |
| **Sum of NPV of Cash Flows (2025-2030)** | | | **\$528.59** |

### Terminal Value Calculation (as of 2030)

$$
\text{Terminal Value}_{2030} = \frac{\text{FCFE}_{2030} \times (1 + \text{Maturity Rate})}{\text{Discount Rate} - \text{Maturity Rate}}
$$

$$
\text{Terminal Value}_{2030} = \frac{\$121.51 \times (1 + 0.01)}{0.08 - 0.01} = \frac{\$122.73}{0.07} = \$1,753.29 \text{ million}
$$

### NPV of Terminal Value (Today)

$$
\text{NPV of Terminal Value} = \text{Terminal Value}_{2030} \times \text{Discount Factor}_{2030}
$$

$$
\text{NPV of Terminal Value} = \$1,753.29 \times 0.6302 = \$1,105.90 \text{ million}
$$

### Total Equity Value (NPV)

$$
\text{Total NPV} = \text{Sum of NPV of Cash Flows} + \text{NPV of Terminal Value}
$$

$$
\text{Total NPV} = \$528.59 + \$1,105.90 = \mathbf{\$1,634.49} \text{ million}
$$

### Justification of Divergence

The calculated Fair Value of **\$66.16** is approximately **7.0% lower** than the current market price of \$71.15. This small divergence suggests that the market's underlying assumptions are only slightly more optimistic than this conservative DCF model.

**Where the Market's Assumptions Differ:**

The market is likely incorporating one or more of the following, which justifies the $\sim 7.0\%$ premium:

1.  **Higher Terminal Growth Rate:** The market may be using a slightly less conservative terminal growth rate, perhaps **1.5%** or **2.0%**, reflecting a belief that Weis Markets will maintain its geographic dominance and that essential grocery retail will grow at a rate closer to GDP. If the terminal rate were increased to 1.5% (still conservative), the fair value would rise to approximately \$71.49, closely aligning with the current market price.

2.  **Higher Near-Term Revenue/Margin Growth:** The market may be assuming the high-growth initiatives, particularly the **46% e-commerce growth** and the capital expenditure program (new stores/conversions), will provide a sustained revenue boost (e.g., $4\%$ growth in 2025 vs. the $3\%$ I projected) and better operating leverage, translating to a higher Net Income margin (e.g., $2.2\%$ vs. my $2.1\%$).

3.  **Lower Discount Rate (WACC):** Given the company's "strong balance sheet" and low debt levels, the market may be assigning a lower risk profile and thus a lower discount rate (WACC), perhaps 7.5% or lower.

**Conclusion on Assumptions:**
My valuation is based on very conservative inputs: a low ROIC of 4.50%, a stable but only marginally improving margin of 2.10%, and a minimal terminal growth rate of 1.0%. The market's valuation is only slightly higher, indicating that it generally agrees with the company's low-risk, slow-growth profile but is baking in a modest, yet justifiable, premium based on a slightly higher terminal growth rate (likely **1.5%**) for this mature, essential consumer defensive business.
