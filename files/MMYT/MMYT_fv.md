### Justification for the Difference

The calculated Fair Value of **$19.68** is significantly lower than the current market price of **$56.25**. This suggests that the market is making far more aggressive assumptions about MMYT's future growth and/or profitability than this conservative DCF model.

**Reasons for the Valuation Disparity (Why the Market is More Optimistic):**

1.  **Market is Pricing in Higher Growth/Longer Runway:**
    *   **Model Assumption:** Revenue growth is decelerated quickly from 18% (FY26) down to 8% (FY30) with a conservative terminal growth of 2.5%.
    *   **Market Assumption:** The market is likely pricing in a **20-25% growth rate** for the next 5+ years, reflecting the overall high growth and massive potential of the Indian travel market, driven by rising disposable incomes and the shift from unorganized to organized/online booking. A more aggressive model with 20% growth for the first three years and a 4.0% terminal rate would yield a higher value.

2.  **Market is Using Higher Long-Term Margins:**
    *   **Model Assumption:** A conservative long-term Net Income Margin of **6.0%**.
    *   **Market Assumption:** The market may be assuming the company can achieve margins in line with more mature global OTA peers like **Booking Holdings (PCLN)** or **Expedia (EXPE)**, whose operating margins are substantially higher. If MMYT's Adjusted Operating Margin (currently ~1.82% of Gross Bookings) can translate into a 10%-12% Net Income Margin in the long term through aggressive operating leverage, the valuation would increase substantially. The market is betting on the high-margin segment (Hotels/Packages/Ancillaries) becoming a much larger part of the mix, leading to massive margin expansion.

3.  **Market Valuation of Cash and Debt (Non-Cash vs. Cash Flow):**
    *   The market is likely ignoring the non-cash interest accounting effect on reported Net Income and valuing the company based on a significantly higher Free Cash Flow to Equity (FCFE) that more closely resembles the Adjusted Net Profit ($51.4M in Q3 FY26 alone).
    *   The market is also implicitly placing a higher value on the **$830M of Cash** by assuming a higher deployment ROIC or a strategic value to the cash horde for acquisitions.

**Conclusion:**
The conservative model provides a large Margin of Safety and indicates that the current stock price implies a scenario of sustained, high-double-digit growth (20%+) and a much higher long-term profitability (10%+ Net Margin) than our conservative assumption of 6.0%. **The market is clearly valuing MMYT as a compounding high-growth technology stock in a massive emerging market (India), betting on a long runway of market share capture and margin expansion over the next decade.** Our valuation is fundamentally different from the market's due to the mandated conservatism in growth and margin assumptions.
