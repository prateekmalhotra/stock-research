2.  **Same-Store Sales Growth (SSSG) & Digital Penetration:** Driving transaction volume via delivery, digital membership, and value promotions.

This is a very conservative ramp-down, acknowledging macro-headwinds and the eventual saturation of key markets.

### **Justification for Difference**

The calculated **Fair Value per Share of \$49.01** is remarkably close to the **Current Market Value of \$49.42**. This minimal difference (less than 1%) suggests that the market's current consensus view is largely aligned with the conservative fundamental assumptions used in this DCF model.

**Market Assumptions Implied by the Valuation:**

1.  **Conservative Growth & SSSG Pressure:** The market is likely pricing in the aggressive new unit expansion (1,600+ new stores per year) but simultaneously modeling low to negative Same-Store Sales Growth (SSSG) due to macroeconomic headwinds in China and increasing competition (e.g., value focus, high-tech local rivals). My model's conservative 5.0% revenue growth for 2025 and subsequent decline, which implies negative SSSG, closely mirrors this reality.
2.  **Stable, High-Quality Margins:** The valuation relies on a stable and only modestly improving net income margin (8.10% to 8.60%). This suggests the market is confident in YUMC's ability to maintain its operational efficiency and G&A cost control (as guided by management to slightly decrease as a percentage of revenue) despite inflationary pressures and the need to offer value to consumers.
3.  **Moderate Reinvestment Returns:** The market appears to be discounting future cash flows with a moderate risk profile. The **10.0% Discount Rate** used and the **8.0% ROIC** on reinvested net income suggests the market believes YUMC is a high-quality, high-cash-flow business in a moderate-risk environment, which is a key assumption for an established market leader in a growing (but volatile) market like China.
4.  **Full Value of Net Cash:** The market is giving full credit for the large net cash position (\$2.7B net cash, or \$5.0B gross cash) on the balance sheet, which is a major component of the final share price.
