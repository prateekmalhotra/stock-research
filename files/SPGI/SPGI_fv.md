### **II. Revenue Engine and Financial Forecast (2025 - 2030)**

S&P Global's business engine is robust, characterized by a mix of highly recurring, high-margin data/subscription revenues (Market Intelligence, Commodity Insights, Indices) and high-growth, cyclical, transaction-based revenues (Ratings).

#### **A. Revenue Engine Justification**

The core of S&P Global's valuation lies in its segment drivers:
1.  **S&P Global Ratings (Transactional/Cyclical):** Revenue is highly dependent on debt issuance volume, which benefits from favorable financing environments and strong investor demand. The Q3 2025 commentary noted strong growth, with Ratings revenue up 12% year-over-year [cite: 6 in step 2].
2.  **S&P Dow Jones Indices (Asset-Linked/Recurring):** Revenue is driven by equity market performance (Asset-Linked Fees) and derivatives trading volume. The base assumption for the market's remaining part of 2025 was "flat" equity markets, which is a *conservative* management view [cite: 6 in step 2].
3.  **S&P Global Market Intelligence & Commodity Insights (Subscription/Recurring):** These segments are more stable and benefit from cross-selling, pricing power, and increased demand for data and analytics. Margin expansion in MI was significant (360 bps in Q3 2025) [cite: 6 in step 2], and the company is actively expanding through acquisitions (e.g., With Intelligence). The Mobility segment spin-off will slightly reduce the topline but increase focus and core margin.

*   **2029-2030 (Pre-Terminal):** Growth decelerates as the company matures further, but still maintains a premium growth rate due to its indispensable market position and pricing power. The rate is set at **5.0%**.

#### **C. Terminal Value Calculation (as of End of 2030)**

The Terminal Value (TV) is the present value of all cash flows after 2030, discounted back to the end of 2030.

$TV_{2030} = FCF_{2030} * (1 + g) / (WACC - g)$
$TV_{2030} = 10,310 * (1 + 0.025) / (0.11 - 0.025) = 10,568 / 0.085$
$TV_{2030} = \$124,329$ million

#### **D. Present Value of Terminal Value**

The TV is then discounted back to the present (Year 0, beginning of 2025).

$NPV\_TV = TV_{2030} / (1 + WACC)^6$
$NPV\_TV = 124,329 / (1 + 0.11)^6$
$NPV\_TV = 124,329 / 1.8704$
$NPV\_TV = \$66,472$ million

#### **Justification for Difference Between Fair Value and Market Price**

My calculated conservative fair value of **$290.69** is significantly lower than the assumed market price (e.g., \$477.59). This large divergence indicates that the market is making much more aggressive assumptions about S&P Global's future growth and profitability than my conservative model.

**Reasons for the Market's Higher Valuation (and the conservatism of my model):**

1.  **More Aggressive Long-Term Growth Rate:** The market is likely pricing in a higher terminal growth rate than my conservative **2.5%**. For a company that is an essential, high-quality, recurring revenue data provider with significant pricing power and market share dominance (Ratings, Indices), the market may assume long-term organic revenue growth of **4.0% to 5.0%** (i.e., higher than my 5.0% in 2030).
    *   *If g = 4.0% was used (with WACC=11.0%), the Fair Value would rise to $\sim\$404.00$.*
2.  **Higher ROIC/Reinvestment Success:** The market believes S&P Global will generate significantly higher returns on its retained earnings and capital expenditures than my conservative **8.0%** ROIC. High-quality businesses often generate ROIC in the high-teens or low-twenties. The market is giving management more credit for its capital allocation.
3.  **Lower Discount Rate (WACC):** The market, viewing SPGI as a very high-quality, defensive asset with low business risk, is likely applying a lower WACC (e.g., 9.0% or 9.5%) than my conservative **11.0%** WACC.
4.  **Optimistic Ratings Cycle:** My model assumes a normalization in the highly profitable Ratings business immediately after 2025. The market may be forecasting a longer, stronger global debt issuance cycle, leading to higher revenue and margin growth for Ratings for more years into the future.

**Conclusion on Discrepancy:**
My conservative assumptions—a rapid slowdown in growth from 7.5% to 5.0% by 2029, a low 8.0% ROIC for a premier franchise, and a high 11.0% discount rate—are responsible for the lower fair value. The market is assigning a premium to SPGI's **quality, pricing power, and defensive nature** (subscription/index revenue floors), and is forecasting a much more optimistic outlook for global capital market activity (Ratings) and the company's ability to maintain high growth rates and high returns for longer. The difference highlights the market's high conviction that S&P Global will operate as an excellent growth company for the foreseeable future.
