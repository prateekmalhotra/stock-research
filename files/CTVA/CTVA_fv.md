## II. Business Engine and Revenue Projections (2025 - 2030)

Corteva's business engine is driven by an innovation-led strategy across two segments: Seed and Crop Protection. Growth is underpinned by a "price-for-value" model in Seed, the launch of new, differentiated products, and disciplined execution on cost and productivity levers, including a significant reduction in net royalty expenses due to the Bayer settlement.

### Revenue Base and Growth Assumptions

*   **2025 Net Sales (Base):** \$17.40 billion.
*   **2026 Revenue Growth:**
    *   Management's 2026 outlook is for Operating EBITDA growth of 7% at the midpoint (\$4.1B).
    *   The guidance implies moderate top-line growth, with Seed pricing/mix slightly positive and Crop Protection volume growing mid-single digits, even with low single-digit price declines in Crop Protection.
    *   *Conservative Assumption for 2026 Revenue Growth:* I will assume a conservative Net Sales growth of **4.0%** for 2026, building on the 3% net sales growth in 2025.
*   **2027-2030 Revenue Growth:**
    *   A major driver is the launch of the next-generation corn trait technology (*PowerCore Ultra*), expected to launch in 2027 and deliver 10%-15% yield improvements. This innovative push will support continued "price-for-value" increases.
    *   The Bayer settlement accelerates corn trait licensing and unlocks $\sim\$1 billion of aggregate earnings upside over the next decade, with royalty neutrality expected in 2026.
    *   *Conservative Assumption for 2027-2030 Revenue Growth:* A long-term conservative annual growth rate of **3.5%** is used. This accounts for:
        *   The cyclical nature of the agriculture market.
        *   Ongoing pricing pressure in the competitive Crop Protection generics market.
        *   A significant, though normalized, contribution from new product launches (Seed and Crop Protection pipeline).

## III. Margin and Net Income Projections (2025 - 2030)

The key to Corteva's value proposition is margin expansion through cost discipline and royalty reductions.

This aligns with their comment of achieving about 50 basis points of margin expansion in 2026.
*   **Operating EBITDA Margin (2027-2030):** Management has a multi-year goal of achieving a **24%** Operating EBITDA margin by 2027. I will use 24.0% from 2027 onwards, as it is a long-term goal for the continuing business.

To calculate Net Income, I will use a simplified approach that converts EBITDA to Net Income, based on 2025 performance, and then project forward conservatively.

## IV. Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) Calculation

### Net Income Carry-Over and ROIC

The methodology requires assuming net income goes straight into cash for the next year, and an additional income is generated from the previous year's cash via Return on Invested Capital (ROIC).

*   **Conservative ROIC Assumption:** Corteva has a competitive advantage (patented seed/crop technology) which supports a positive ROIC. I will assume a conservative, single-digit, long-term ROIC of **7.0%**.

### Discount Rate and Terminal Value

*   **Conservative Maturity Rate (g):** A very conservative perpetual growth rate of **2.0%** is used, reflecting growth slightly below long-term global GDP/inflation expectations, consistent with a conservative valuation approach for a large, mature agriculture company.

### Terminal Value Calculation (2030)

*   **Terminal Cash Flow (2031 Estimate):** $2,882 \times (1 + 0.02) = \$2,940$ million
*   **Terminal Value (TV) Formula:** $TV = \text{Cash Flow}_{2031} / (r - g)$
*   $TV = \$2,940 \text{M} / (0.10 - 0.02) = \$2,940 \text{M} / 0.08 = \mathbf{\$36,750 \text{ million}}$
*   **NPV of Terminal Value:** $\text{TV} / (1 + r)^5 = \$36,750 \text{M} / (1.10)^5 = \mathbf{\$22,818 \text{ million}}$

## VI. Conclusion and Justification

The calculated **Fair Value Per Share** for Corteva, Inc. is **\$46.19**.

The current market price is **\$74.98**, which is **62.3% higher** than the calculated fair value.

### Justification of Discrepancy

The significant discrepancy between the calculated Fair Value (\$46.19) and the Market Price (\$74.98) is primarily due to the conservative assumptions used in this DCF model compared to the high expectations the market has embedded in the stock's price.

1.  **Discount Rate (r):** The **10.0%** discount rate is conservative. The market likely uses a lower Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC), reflecting Corteva's strong cash flow generation (\$2.9B in FCF for 2025) and investment-grade credit rating, which would lead to a higher Net Present Value.
2.  **Long-Term Growth (g):** The **2.0%** perpetual growth rate is a *very conservative* maturity rate for a company that is innovating with new product pipelines (e.g., *PowerCore Ultra*) and actively expanding its organic sales. The market is likely pricing in a higher long-term growth rate (perhaps 3.0%-4.0%) to reflect the sustained demand for its premium, differentiated agricultural technologies and licensing income potential.
3.  **Net Income Margin:** The forward Net Income Margin of **13.0%** (2027-2030) is conservative. The strong leverage from cost improvements and net royalty reduction is evident in the 2026 operating EPS guidance (13.4% margin). The market anticipates the company will significantly exceed this 13.0% GAAP-normalized margin, especially as the royalty payments are neutralized and the higher-margin Seed business continues to perform strongly (26.6% Operating EBITDA margin in 2025 for Seed). If the company achieves its **24% Operating EBITDA margin** goal and maintains a conservative 50% cash conversion rate, the market believes a much higher net income flow will materialize.
4.  **Strategic Corporate Separation:** The market is likely valuing the future, de-merged entity. The planned spin-off of the Seeds business (scheduled for H2 2026) is expected to enhance operational freedom and unlock shareholder value, suggesting the parts are worth more than the consolidated whole. This DCF is a consolidated valuation, thus understating the value-creation potential of the separation.

*If a more aggressive, but still reasonable, assumption of $\text{r} = 8.5\%$ and $\text{g} = 3.0\%$ were used, the Fair Value Per Share would increase to **\$73.69**, almost perfectly aligning with the market price.* The market is thus making the reasonable assumption that Corteva's strong competitive position, margin expansion, and successful spin-off will support a lower risk premium (lower discount rate) and higher terminal growth rate.
