### 3. Net Present Value (NPV) Calculation

#### **A. Return on Invested Capital (ROIC) Assumption**

*   **2024 ROCE (proxy for ROIC):** 14.7%
*   **ROIC Assumption:** A conservative figure of **7.5%** is used. This is deliberately much lower than the reported 2024 ROCE of 14.7% to reflect a cautious, long-term assumption and mitigate risk, especially following a year of negative organic growth.

The cash flow assumption is as follows: $\text{Cash Flow}_{\text{year}}$ = $\text{Net Income}_{\text{year}}$ (from Projection Table) + $\text{ROIC}_{\text{assumed}} \times \text{Net Income}_{\text{previous year}}$

#### **B. Discount Rate and Terminal Value**

*   **Discount Rate (Conservative but reasonable):** **10.0%**. This rate is used to reflect the risk of a global industrial company, higher than the risk-free rate and incorporating an equity risk premium.
*   **Maturity Rate (Conservative):** **2.0%**. This long-term growth rate for the terminal value is conservative and well below the projected 5.0% growth and the company's long-term macro tailwinds.

The Terminal Value (TV) is calculated using the Perpetuity Growth Model:

$\text{TV} = \text{Cash Flow}_{\text{2030}} \times (1 + \text{Maturity Rate}) / (\text{Discount Rate} - \text{Maturity Rate})$
$\text{TV} = 339.2 \times (1 + 0.02) / (0.10 - 0.02)$
$\text{TV} = 345.984 / 0.08$
$\text{TV} = \text{EUR 4,324.8 Million}$

**Net Present Value of Terminal Value:**

$\text{NPV}_{\text{TV}} = \text{TV} \times \text{Discount Factor}_{\text{2030}}$
$\text{NPV}_{\text{TV}} = 4,324.8 \times 0.564$
$\text{NPV}_{\text{TV}} = \text{EUR 2,439.1 Million}$

**Total Net Present Value (NPV):**

$\text{Total NPV} = \text{NPV}_{\text{Project Period}} + \text{NPV}_{\text{TV}}$
$\text{Total NPV} = 1,243.2 + 2,439.1$
$\text{Total NPV} = \text{EUR 3,682.3 Million}$

### 4. Fair Value Calculation and Justification

**Fair Value per Share Calculation:**

$\text{Fair Value} = (\text{Total NPV} - \text{Net Debt}) / \text{Shares Outstanding}$
$\text{Fair Value} = (3,682.3 - 597) / 108.04$
$\text{Fair Value} = 3,085.3 / 108.04$
$\text{Fair Value} = \text{EUR 28.56 per share}$

#### **Justification of Valuation and Market Assumptions**

The calculated Fair Value of **EUR 28.56** is almost identical to the current market price of EUR 28.34, suggesting that the market's assumptions are closely aligned with the conservative model constructed here.

**Alignment with Market Assumptions:**

The valuation is highly sensitive to the future growth rate and the discount rate. The fact that the valuation is a near-perfect match indicates the market is essentially pricing in:

1.  **A Conservative but Steady Recovery:** The market is agreeing with the management's guidance of a flat 2025 organic revenue and a subsequent recovery to a mid-single-digit (5.0%) organic growth rate for the long term, which aligns with the company's 'thrive 2030' strategy and macro tailwinds.
2.  **Modest Margin Improvement:** The slight, gradual improvement of the Net Income Margin from 5.95% to 7.50% is perceived as achievable and sustainable but not overly optimistic, reflecting a cautious view on the profitability benefit from the operational excellence programs.
3.  **Required Return:** The market's implicit Discount Rate (Cost of Equity/WACC) is very close to the assumed conservative **10.0%**, indicating a realistic assessment of the company's risk profile within the industrial sector.
4.  **ROIC Discipline:** The market is not projecting a significant boost to Net Income from capital reinvestment, as reflected by the use of a conservative **7.5% ROIC** compared to the 2024 ROCE of 14.7%. The market is valuing the cash flow primarily from operations rather than aggressive, high-return reinvestment.

In conclusion, the market is making **conservative but realistic assumptions** about Aalberts N.V.'s ability to execute its 'thrive 2030' strategy by navigating short-term cyclical headwinds (2024 organic decline and flat 2025 guidance) and returning to a sustainable, mid-single-digit growth trajectory with moderately improved margins. This DCF model validates the current market price.
