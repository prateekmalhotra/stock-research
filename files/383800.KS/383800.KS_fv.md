## **Part 3: Terminal Value and Fair Value Calculation**

### **Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) Calculation**

#### **Terminal Value (TV) Calculation**

The Terminal Value represents the value of all cash flows beyond the projection period (2030).
*   **Final FCFE ($FCFE_{2030}$):** KRW 221,281 Million
*   **Maturity Rate (g_terminal):** 2.0% (Very conservative, long-term economic growth proxy)
*   **Discount Rate (r):** 9.0%

$TV_{2030} = \frac{FCFE_{2030} \times (1 + g_{\text{terminal}})}{r - g_{\text{terminal}}}$

$TV_{2030} = \frac{221,281 \times (1 + 0.02)}{0.09 - 0.02} = \frac{225,707}{0.07}$

$TV_{2030} = 3,224,386 \text{ Million KRW}$

#### **Net Present Value (NPV) of Terminal Value**

$NPV \text{ of } TV = \frac{TV_{2030}}{(1 + r)^{6}}$

$NPV \text{ of } TV = \frac{3,224,386}{(1.09)^{6}} = \frac{3,224,386}{1.677}$

$NPV \text{ of } TV = 1,922,604 \text{ Million KRW}$

#### **Total Equity Value**

$Total \text{ Equity Value} = NPV \text{ of FCFE}_{2025-2030} + NPV \text{ of } TV + \text{Cash} - \text{Debt}$

$Total \text{ Equity Value} = 870,468 + 1,922,604 + 312,100 - 0$

$Total \text{ Equity Value} = 3,105,172 \text{ Million KRW}$

### **Fair Value Per Share**

$Fair \text{ Value Per Share} = \frac{Total \text{ Equity Value}}{\text{Shares Outstanding}}$

$Fair \text{ Value Per Share} = \frac{3,105,172,000,000 \text{ KRW}}{78,000,000 \text{ shares}}$

$Fair \text{ Value Per Share} = 39,810 \text{ KRW}$

### **Justification for Discrepancy (Fair Value vs. Market Price)**

The calculated Fair Value of **39,810 KRW** is significantly higher (nearly 4.9 times) than the current market price of **8,130 KRW**. This massive discrepancy suggests that the market is making radically different, and much more conservative, assumptions about the company's future value.

#### **Market's Implied Assumptions**

The market appears to be assigning a deep "Holding Company Discount" and applying highly conservative assumptions for growth and cash flow. The key assumptions the market is likely making are:

1.  **Deep Holding Company Discount:** The market often values pure holding companies based on the market value of their listed subsidiaries, then applies a massive discount (e.g., 50-80%) due to the complex corporate structure, minority shareholder risk, and potential for misallocation of capital by the parent. LX Holdings's value is currently trading at a fraction of the theoretical sum of its subsidiaries' parts.
2.  **Zero or Negative Real Growth for Income Drivers:** The market is likely pricing in *zero* or *negative* long-term real growth for the highly cyclical businesses (Commodities, Logistics, and Semiconductors) owned by the subsidiaries. The market fears that:
    *   **LX International's** strong earnings from commodities and logistics (LX Pantos) are temporary/cyclical peaks, and future earnings will normalize downward.
    *   **LX Semicon**'s diversification efforts may fail to generate high-margin growth in the crowded semiconductor market.
3.  **High Discount Rate/WACC:** The market is likely using a much higher implied discount rate than the conservative 9.0% used in this model, reflecting a higher perceived risk due to:
    *   **Geopolitical and Economic Volatility:** South Korea's economic exposure and the volatility of global commodity and chip markets.
    *   **Lack of Liquidity/Transparency:** As an unlisted holding company whose main asset is the stock of a listed subsidiary (LX International, etc.), the cash flows and strategic decisions are less transparent than the subsidiaries themselves.

#### **Justification of DCF Model Assumptions**

The model's $39,810 \text{ KRW}$ Fair Value stands on the following reasonable, justifiable assumptions:

1.  **Conservative Growth Rate:** The chosen **5.0%** Net Income growth rate is **already conservative**. LX International alone is projected to see a massive jump in profitability in the near term, and LX Semicon is entering new, high-growth areas. 5.0% is a modest long-term growth rate for a diversified group of companies with clear strategic expansion.
2.  **Zero Debt & High Cash:** The holding company's **zero debt** position and **high cash reserves** (KRW 312.1B) significantly enhance the FCFE and reduce its intrinsic risk. The market is ignoring this robust balance sheet.
3.  **Conservative Terminal Value:** The **2.0%** maturity rate is in line with the long-term inflation/GDP of a developed economy, which is the definition of a *conservative* terminal growth rate.

**Conclusion on Discrepancy:**

The market is currently pricing LX Holdings Corp. as a low-growth, high-risk entity subject to a punitive holding company discount. The DCF model suggests that the **intrinsic value** is significantly higher, driven by the strong, diversified subsidiary business engines and a clean balance sheet at the holding company level. The massive difference represents the market's current **lack of confidence** in the sustainability of the subsidiaries' earnings and the holding company's ability to unlock or realize the full value of its assets for its shareholders. This suggests the stock is currently **significantly undervalued** based on intrinsic value.
