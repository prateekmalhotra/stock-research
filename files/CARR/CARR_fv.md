**Conclusion:**

My valuation is deliberately conservative, as per the rules, by using a $49.53 fair value, which is below the current market price. The $8.02 difference suggests the market believes Carrier will either achieve the high end of its long-term 6-8% growth target or exceed the conservative margin expansion assumptions due to the significant structural benefits from its portfolio transformation into a more focused, high-growth, and high-margin climate solutions company. I believe my revenue and margin assumptions are reasonable and conservative given the current residential headwinds but the long-term secular tailwinds (Data Center, heat pumps, etc.) which should propel the business into the 6% growth rate. For a purchase decision, one would need to be confident that the company can consistently execute on the higher end of its long-term guidance (7-8% organic growth) and achieve margins well over 11.0% to justify the current stock price.
