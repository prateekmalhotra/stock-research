### **Step 5: Fair Value Calculation**

Fair Value = (Total NPV of Future Cash Flows + Current Cash & Cash Equivalents - Total Debt) / Shares Outstanding

*   Total NPV of Future Cash Flows: **\$253.0 million**
*   Current Cash & Cash Equivalents: **\$46.745 million**
*   Total Debt: **\$213.592 million**
*   Fully Diluted Shares Outstanding: **52.8 million**

$$ \text{Fair Value per Share} = \frac{\$253.0 \text{ M} + \$46.745 \text{ M} - \$213.592 \text{ M}}{52.8 \text{ M Shares}} $$

$$ \text{Fair Value per Share} = \frac{\$86.153 \text{ M}}{52.8 \text{ M Shares}} $$

### **Fair Value Justification and Market Comparison**

**Fair Value Per Share:** **\$1.63**

#### **Justification of the Fair Value**

The calculated fair value of **\$1.63** is approximately **18.5% lower** than the current market price of **\$2.00**.

The valuation is based on a conservative model of the company's turnaround:
1.  **Conservative Debt:** The last available full debt figure was used, which could be higher than the current figure given the company's focus on capital structure, but this ensures a conservative outcome.
2.  **Conservative Growth:** The model projects slow growth, starting with a revenue decline in 2025 to account for the current headwinds and the churn from a large bank partner, even as the new MQUs and new ad platform launch.
3.  **Conservative Profitability:** The Net Income projection is a slow, gradual move to a modest 7.5% net margin by 2030.
4.  **High Discount Rate:** A 10.0% discount rate is used to explicitly factor in the significant execution risk of the company's strategic pivot (engagement-based pricing, new FI-agnostic platform, mitigating partner loss) and its reliance on a limited number of financial institution partners.

#### **Why is the Market Making Different Assumptions?**

The market price of **\$2.00** suggests investors are making a significantly more optimistic set of assumptions about the success of the company's turnaround and its long-term growth potential than my conservative model:

*   **Market's Higher Terminal Value/Growth Expectation:** The market is likely pricing in a substantially higher terminal growth rate (perhaps 3.5% - 4.0%) and/or a much higher long-term net income margin (e.g., 10%-15%+) reflecting a successful platform diversification and realization of economies of scale.
*   **Market's Higher User Monetization (ACPU) Expectation:** Investors are betting that the **engagement-based pricing** and the new products (like Cardlytics Rewards Platform) will quickly reverse the declining ACPU trend and drive significantly higher monetization from the growing MQU base sooner than 2027. They see the 19% MQU growth as the dominant long-term driver, outweighing the short-term monetization dip.
*   **Market's Lower Risk Profile (Lower Discount Rate):** The market's higher price suggests a lower perceived risk (e.g., a 9.0% or 8.0% discount rate) as they believe management's actions have **"derisked"** the business by refinancing the convertible notes and focusing on a diversified supply and advertiser base, making the business engine less reliant on a few legacy bank partners. The recent positive Adjusted EBITDA in Q2 2025 also gives the market confidence that the pivot to profitability is sustainable.

In conclusion, my fair value of **\$1.63** is a **buy** for a long-term, deep-value investor who requires a significant margin of safety and hedges against the company failing to execute its pivot perfectly. The market, however, is willing to pay a premium for the *potential* of the turnaround, especially with the recent positive Adjusted EBITDA and MQU growth trends, suggesting an expectation for faster and more profitable execution than my conservative projections allow.
