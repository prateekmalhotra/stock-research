## **Part 2: Business Engine & Revenue Projection (2025 - 2030)**

### **Business Engine Analysis**

ECG's business is fundamentally driven by **specialty contracting services** across two segments:
1.  **Electrical & Mechanical (E&M):** Driven by high-growth, high-value markets.
2.  **Transmission & Distribution (T&D):** Driven by steady, government/utility-backed infrastructure spending.

The core revenue engine is the conversion of its **Backlog** into revenue, supplemented by new project wins. The key drivers are:

*   **Secular Growth Tailwind:** The E&M segment, the largest portion of revenue (approx. 71% in 2024), is experiencing explosive growth, particularly in the **Data Center/Cloud/AI submarket**. This is cited multiple times by management as a key driver.
*   **Infrastructure Spending:** The T&D segment benefits from national trends in **utility grid modernization, undergrounding, and high-tech reshoring**.
*   **Backlog Visibility:** As of Q2 2025, the backlog stood at **\$3.0 billion** (up 24% YoY), which is a significant multiple of the trailing 12-month revenue and gives management high visibility into future revenue.
*   **Efficiency:** The strategic focus on **off-site manufacturing and prefabrication** is expected to bolster its competitive edge and operational efficiency, leading to margin resilience.

### **Margin Assumptions**

Management noted that 2025 margins would be negatively impacted by approximately **\$28 million** of stand-alone/dis-synergy costs.

*   **Conservative Net Income Margin (2025-2026):** We will project Net Income Margin to be **5.0%** in 2025 (consistent with FY 2024 actual) and 2026, absorbing the temporary stand-up costs. This is conservative as the Q2 2025 Net Income Margin was already 5.7%.
*   **Normalized/Expansion Net Income Margin (2027-2030):** Assuming stand-alone costs are fully absorbed and the focus on high-margin data center work and prefabrication pays off, we project margin expansion to a conservative **5.5%** by 2027 and **6.0%** by 2030.

### **Return on Invested Capital (ROIC) Calculation**

ROIC is used to project additional income generated from retained earnings (the prior year's Net Income).

*   **Invested Capital (FY 2024):** Total Debt (\$292.5M) + Total Equity (\$514.4M from an external source estimate, which is appropriate for ROIC calculation) = **\$806.9 million** (Using the latest debt and an estimate of equity).
*   **ROIC (Trailing 12 Months):** Net Income (TTM $\sim$\$165.75M) / Invested Capital $(\sim$\$806.9M) $\approx$ **20.5%**.
*   **Conservative ROIC Assumption:** We will assume a highly conservative and sustainable **10.0% ROIC** for the valuation, significantly lower than the company's estimated TTM ROIC to account for cyclicality and high capital-intensive nature of the construction business.

## **Part 4: Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) Valuation**

### **Discount Rate Assumptions**

*   **Conservative Discount Rate (WACC proxy):** **9.5%**. This is a conservative yet reasonable rate for a construction/specialty contractor with high growth (15-20% near term, moderating to 8-10% long term) and a good balance sheet (Net Leverage 0.8x).
*   **Conservative Maturity/Terminal Growth Rate:** **3.0%**. This is a conservative assumption, slightly above long-term inflation/GDP growth, reflecting the company's strong positioning in secularly growing markets (e.g., data centers, utility infrastructure).

### **Terminal Value Calculation (2030)**

The cash flow in 2030 ($\text{CF}_{2030}$) is the Total Net Income projected for 2030: \$496.5 million.

Terminal Value in 2030 ($\text{TV}_{2030}$) = $\text{CF}_{2030} \times (1 + \text{Growth Rate}) / (\text{Discount Rate} - \text{Growth Rate})$

$\text{TV}_{2030}$ = $\$496.5 \times (1 + 0.03) / (0.095 - 0.03)$
$\text{TV}_{2030}$ = $\$496.5 \times 1.03 / 0.065$
$\text{TV}_{2030}$ = $\mathbf{\$7,858.9}$ **million**

## **Part 6: Conclusion and Market Analysis**

**Calculated Fair Value:** **\$112.23 per share**

**Current Market Price:** **\$91.00 per share**

### **Justification for Difference**

The calculated Fair Value of **\$112.23** is approximately **23.3% higher** than the current market price of \$91.00.

The market is making a few conservative assumptions that differ from the base case model, primarily related to:

1.  **Slower Backlog Conversion/Sustainability of High Growth:** The market may be discounting the sustainability of the 15%+ growth rate in 2025 and 2026. While the \$3.0B backlog is strong, a slower conversion cycle due to the complex, multi-year nature of new projects (as mentioned in commentary) may be anticipated by investors.
2.  **Margin Normalization:** The model assumes an ultimate margin expansion to 6.0% net income by 2030, driven by operational efficiencies (pre-fabrication) and the favorable project mix (data centers). The market, observing the initial drag from public company stand-up costs (\$28M) and historical volatility in construction margins, may be baking in a more modest long-term net income margin (e.g., closer to 5.0% - 5.5%).
3.  **Conservative Terminal Value:** A slight downward adjustment in the market's perceived Terminal Growth Rate (e.g., 2.5% instead of 3.0%) or a higher long-term WACC (e.g., 10% instead of 9.5%) could easily bridge this valuation gap.
