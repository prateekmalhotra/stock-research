## III. Terminal Value & Discounted Cash Flow

### A. Terminal Value Calculation (as of 2030)

Terminal Value (TV) represents the value of all cash flows after 2030, calculated using the Perpetuity Growth Model (Gordon Growth Model).

*   **TV Formula:** [Final Year Cash Flow * (1 + g)] / (WACC - g)
    *   Final Year Cash Flow: $610,497 thousand
    *   Conservative Maturity Rate (g): 2.0%
    *   Conservative Discount Rate (WACC): 10.0%

$TV_{2030}$ = [$610,497 * (1 + 0.02)]$ / $(0.10 - 0.02)$
$TV_{2030}$ = $622,707$ / $0.08$
$TV_{2030}$ = **$7,783,838 thousand** (or **$7.784 Billion**)

### B. Terminal Value Net Present Value

The Terminal Value is discounted back to the present (2024 end) using the 2030 Discount Factor.

*   $NPV_{TV}$ = $TV_{2030}$ * Discount Factor (2030)
*   $NPV_{TV}$ = $7,783,838$ * $0.564$
*   $NPV_{TV}$ = **$4,388,579 thousand** (or **$4.389 Billion**)

### C. Total Net Present Value (Enterprise Value)

Total NPV = NPV of Projected Cash Flows (2025-2030) + NPV of Terminal Value

*   Total NPV = $2,250,241$ thousand + $4,388,579$ thousand
*   **Total NPV (Enterprise Value)** = **$6,638,820 thousand** (or **$6.639 Billion**)

The calculated Fair Value of **$193.00** is significantly higher than the market price of **$86.01**. This large discrepancy is primarily due to the conservative assumptions required by the model in the context of a bank's financial structure:

1.  **Bank Valuation and Free Cash Flow:** The model's instruction to treat **Net Income as Free Cash Flow (FCF)** for DCF is a highly generous assumption for a financial institution. Banks must retain a significant portion of their Net Income to meet regulatory capital requirements (e.g., CET1 ratio was 12.1% in Q3 2025) and fund future loan growth. Therefore, the "FCF" available to shareholders is a much smaller number (Excess Earnings) than the entire Net Income, leading to a massive overestimation of the terminal value.

The market's implied long-term profit margin is much lower than the 44.0% PPNR margin achieved in Q3 2025.
3.  **Conservative WACC:** Our 10.0% Discount Rate (WACC) is conservative but still lower than the rate a skeptical market might apply to a regional bank with a history of strategic transformation and recent credit challenges.

**Conclusion:** The model's Fair Value of **$193.00** represents a **maximum theoretical value** assuming the bank is a typical corporation where all Net Income is available to shareholders, the strong Q3 2025 margins are sustained, and the low-risk ROIC is maintained for the next decade.
