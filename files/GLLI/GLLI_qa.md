## Business Summary

Globalink Investment Inc. is a Special Purpose Acquisition Company (SPAC), meaning its sole business is to raise money from public investors (its primary capital source) and use those funds to merge with and take a promising private company public. GLLI itself generates no revenue from selling goods or services, and therefore has no traditional customer base or product distribution to describe. The operating model is purely transactional; its success depends entirely on identifying and closing an acquisition of a suitable target company within a limited time frame, usually 18 to 24 months.

---

## Business Model Risk

The most significant near-term risk is the acquisition deadline; if GLLI fails to complete a merger within its required timeframe, it must dissolve and return capital to investors, resulting in a failed enterprise. Long-term risks relate primarily to the quality and valuation of the eventual target company; if the acquired company is overpriced or fails to meet projected earnings after the merger, GLLI shareholders will suffer losses. The heightened skepticism toward SPACs since 2021 is a major current concern, as this market shift often leads to high investor redemptions before the merger closes, severely reducing the amount of cash available for the acquired business to fund its future growth and operations.

## Company History

This history lesson is structured to explain the journey of Globalink Investment Inc. (GLLI), a company type known as a Special Purpose Acquisition Company (SPAC), in simple terms.

### A History of Globalink Investment Inc. (GLLI)

#### The Founding and Core Goal

1.  **Inception as a "Blank Check" Company (2021):** Globalink Investment Inc. (GLLI) was established in March 2021. It was not a traditional company with an existing product or service; instead, it was a "blank check company," or a Special Purpose Acquisition Company (SPAC).
2.  **The Business's Purpose:** The sole purpose of a SPAC like GLLI is to raise money from investors and then use that money to find and buy a private operating company, effectively bringing that private company public much faster than a traditional Initial Public Offering (IPO).
3.  **Initial Public Offering (IPO):** GLLI completed its IPO in December 2021, raising a significant amount of money that was placed into a trust account, waiting to be deployed for the acquisition.
4.  **Acquisition Target Focus:** The GLLI team, led by its executives, planned to look for a promising business in the **medical technology** and **green energy** sectors, primarily in North America, Europe, and parts of Asia (excluding China and Hong Kong).

#### Headwinds and Challenges Along the Way

5.  **The Time Constraint Challenge:** By law, SPACs have a limited amount of time (typically 18-24 months) to complete an acquisition, or they must liquidate and return the money to their investors; this deadline pressure was a constant headwind.
6.  **Failed Initial Attempt:** The company faced an early setback when a previously announced merger deal with an initial target, Tomorrow Crypto Group, was terminated in March 2023.
7.  **Regulatory and Compliance Issues:** A significant challenge was encountered when GLLI's stock was taken off (delisted from) the Nasdaq stock exchange because the company did not comply with certain listing rules.
8.  **Financial Strain:** Throughout the process, the company experienced increased financial challenges, reporting a net loss and dealing with limited cash for operations (liquidity constraints) while trying to finalize a deal.

#### Innovative Solutions and Tailwind Strategies

9.  **Securing a New Target (Tailwind/Solution):** GLLI successfully pivoted from its failed initial deal and signed a definitive merger agreement with **Alps Global Holding Berhad** in January 2024, a company focused on biotechnology and healthcare.
10. **The Extension Tactic (Solution):** To combat the strict time constraint, GLLI repeatedly extended its deadline to complete the merger, a strategic move that typically requires making additional cash deposits into the trust account to gain more time.
11. **Flexibility to Close the Deal (Solution):** The company repeatedly amended the merger agreement with Alps, including removing certain financial conditions, which demonstrated flexibility and a strong commitment to making the acquisition work and getting it across the finish line.
12. **The Biotech Sector Opportunity (Tailwind):** The chosen target, Alps, was in the high-growth sector of predictive and preventive medicine, specifically biotechnology, which offered a strong, timely investment narrative for GLLI’s shareholders.

#### The Present Date: Mission Accomplished

13. **Successful Business Combination (Reverse Merger):** On October 31, 2025, Globalink Investment Inc. successfully completed its long-sought business combination with Alps Group, a process often called a "reverse merger."
14. **The New Public Company:** Upon closing the deal, the original SPAC (GLLI) ceased to be a standalone entity. The combined public company is now called **Alps Group Inc.** and its shares began trading under the new stock symbol "ALPS."
15. **The Final Goal:** By completing the merger, GLLI successfully fulfilled its entire founding purpose: it used the money it raised from the public market to acquire a private company, giving that company a path to a public listing on the Nasdaq.
