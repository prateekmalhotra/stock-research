## **IV. Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) Calculation**

### **Assumptions for DCF:**

*   **Conservative Discount Rate (WACC Proxy):** **10.0%** (Reflects the company's higher volatility and cost of debt).
*   **Conservative Maturity Rate (Perpetual Growth Rate):** **2.5%** (A conservative perpetual growth rate reflecting long-term GDP growth plus the structural tailwind from environmental regulation).

### **Terminal Value Calculation (2030):**

Using the Gordon Growth Model, where Terminal Value is the cash flow for 2030 growing forever:

$$
\text{Terminal Value} = \frac{\text{Cash Flow}_{2030} \times (1 + \text{Maturity Rate})}{\text{Discount Rate} - \text{Maturity Rate}}
$$

$$
\text{Terminal Value} = \frac{\text{\$127.7M} \times (1 + 0.025)}{0.10 - 0.025} = \frac{\text{\$130.89M}}{0.075} \approx \text{\$1,745.2M}
$$

**NPV of Terminal Value:**

$$
\text{NPV of Terminal Value} = \frac{\text{\$1,745.2M}}{(1 + 0.10)^6} \approx \text{\$985.3M}
$$

The sum of the projected cash flow NPVs is **\$380.7M**.
**Total Enterprise Value (NPV of all future cash flows):** $\text{\$380.7M} + \text{\$985.3M} = \mathbf{\$1,366.0M}$

***

## **V. Fair Value Calculation**

$$
\text{Fair Value of Equity} = \text{Total Enterprise Value} + \text{Total Cash} - \text{Total Debt}
$$

$$
\text{Fair Value of Equity} = \text{\$1,366.0M} + \text{\$36.8M} - \text{\$235.8M} = \mathbf{\$1,167.0M}
$$

$$
\text{Fair Value per Share} = \frac{\text{Fair Value of Equity}}{\text{Shares Outstanding}}
$$

$$
\text{Fair Value per Share} = \frac{\text{\$1,167.0M}}{35.64 \text{ million shares}} \approx \mathbf{\$32.74}
$$

# **Conclusion and Justification**

### **Fair Value of CECO Stock: \$32.74**

The calculated fair value of **\$32.74 per share** is significantly lower than the current market price of approximately **\$53.30 per share**.

### **Justification for the Valuation Discrepancy**

The market is making significantly more optimistic assumptions than this conservative DCF model, primarily in two key areas: **Future Growth Rate (The Business Engine)** and **Margin Expansion**.

1.  **More Aggressive Revenue Growth (The Market's Assumption):**
    *   This model projects revenue to grow from \$900M in 2026 to \$1.2B in 2030 (a CAGR of 7.5%).
    *   To justify a \$53.30 stock price, the market is likely factoring in a **much longer period of double-digit growth**, extrapolating the recent success and high backlog growth well beyond 2026. The market is likely assuming that CECO's programmatic M&A strategy will accelerate growth and that the global regulatory tailwind for environmental solutions is stronger and more durable than the conservative 5-8% growth used after 2026.

2.  **Higher Terminal Value Assumption (Lower Discount Rate or Higher Perpetual Growth):**
    *   This model uses a 10.0% Discount Rate (WACC proxy) and a 2.5% Perpetual Growth Rate.
    *   If the market believes CECO's structural improvements make it a less risky business (a more stable, recurring service component to the revenue base), it would use a **lower Discount Rate** (e.g., 8.5%), which would dramatically increase the Net Present Value of the Terminal Value.
    *   Furthermore, if the market assumes a higher Perpetual Growth Rate (e.g., **4.0%**), reflecting an expectation that environmental issues will drive long-term sector growth well above GDP, the fair value would increase to approximately **\$48.00 - \$50.00**, largely closing the gap with the current price.

**Conclusion:**

My valuation is conservative because it strictly adheres to a step-down in growth after the management-guided 2026, assumes a high-risk discount rate (10.0%), and uses a very conservative Perpetual Growth Rate (2.5%). The market is likely pricing CECO as a company that has successfully *structurally transformed* into a high-quality environmental growth asset, justifying a higher growth rate assumption (4.0%+) into perpetuity and a lower WACC (8.5%-9.0%) than used in this analysis. **The market is valuing the company's future M&A and structural margin improvements more richly.**
