### 6. Justification and Market Interpretation

#### Justification for Discrepancy

My calculated Fair Value per Share of **$35.91** is significantly lower than the Current Market Price of **$59.00**. This suggests the market is making more aggressive assumptions about Dominion Energy's future growth and profitability, or is valuing the stock on a different metric.

**The primary reasons for the large difference are:**

1.  **Discount Rate:** The market may be using a lower cost of equity/WACC than my conservative **7.0%**. A lower discount rate is often applied to high-quality regulated utilities, especially those with a massive, long-duration capital plan. If the market is using a **5.5%** discount rate with a $2.5\%$ maturity rate, the Terminal Value alone nearly doubles, and the Fair Value jumps to approximately **$60.00**.
    *   **Market Implied Discount Rate:** The market appears to be pricing D at a WACC closer to **5.5% - 6.0%** to justify its current stock price.

2.  **The Earnings Base and Growth Rate:** My projection starts from a conservative application of the GAAP Net Income Margin on the projected *new* base earnings. While I used the conservative end of the $5\%$-$7\%$ EPS growth (i.e., $5\%$), the market may be pricing in the high end, or even factoring in an accelerated growth due to the immense **data center demand** in Northern Virginia.
    *   **Data Center Impact:** Management explicitly highlighted that data centers now account for **$\approx 26\%$ of Dominion Energy Virginia's electric load** and that contracted capacity has jumped from 21 GW to **40 GW** in a few months (FY 2024 Annual Report). This monumental, *guaranteed* load growth is a massive de-risking factor and a catalyst that may lead the market to assume **$7\%$ or greater** EPS growth will continue well past 2029, or that the **$50 Billion** capex plan will be expanded and accelerated.

3.  **Use of Operating Earnings vs. GAAP Net Income:** Utilities are often valued based on their non-GAAP Operating Earnings, which exclude volatile items like mark-to-market adjustments. My Net Income Base for 2025 is derived directly from the **Operating EPS guidance of $3.40**, which is what management uses as their key metric. The market likely ignores the GAAP volatility and is solely focused on the highly predictable Operating EPS/Cash Flow stream growing at $5\%$-$7\%$ forever.

**Conclusion:** My valuation is conservative because I used the low end of management's growth guidance and a higher-than-market discount rate for a utility. The market is pricing Dominion Energy as a utility with a significantly lower risk profile (lower WACC of $\sim 5.5\%$) and a guaranteed, high-end growth trajectory due to the unparalleled Virginia data center market. I will stand by the conservative valuation of **$35.91** as my fair value, noting that the market is factoring in a lower cost of capital and higher terminal growth fueled by the data center secular trend.
