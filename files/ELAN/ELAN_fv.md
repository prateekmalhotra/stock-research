### **B. Discount Rate and Terminal Value**

*   **Discount Rate (Conservative but Reasonable):** I will use **8.0%**. This is a reasonable cost of capital for a large, established animal health company with a diversified portfolio, but it is conservative given the company's recent historical volatility and debt load.
*   **Maturity Rate (Conservative Terminal Growth):** I will use **2.0%**. This reflects a long-term growth rate slightly above global inflation for a mature pharmaceutical company operating in a stable, necessary market (animal health is defensive and tied to pet/livestock populations). This is very conservative.

**Terminal Value (TV) Calculation (2030):**
*   TV = [2030 Cash Flow * (1 + Maturity Rate)] / (Discount Rate - Maturity Rate)
*   TV = [699.3 * (1 + 0.02)] / (0.08 - 0.02)
*   TV = 713.29 / 0.06
*   **Terminal Value = \$11,888.17 Million**

## **V. Conclusion and Justification**

**Fair Value Per Share: \$17.66**

**Current Market Price: \$22.85**

The calculated fair value of **\$17.66 per share** is **22.7% lower** than the current market price of \$22.85.

### **Market Assumptions vs. Valuation Assumptions**

The market is making a clear assumption that is more optimistic than my conservative DCF model. The market is likely pricing in:

1.  **Higher Terminal Growth:** The market may be assuming a maturity rate greater than my conservative 2.0%, perhaps 3.0% to 3.5%, reflecting the "defensive growth" nature of the animal health sector and the success of key competitor Zoetis. A higher terminal growth rate dramatically increases the Terminal Value, as the DCF is highly sensitive to this number.
    *   *Example:* If the Maturity Rate were 3.0%, the Fair Value per Share would increase to approximately **\$20.01**.
2.  **Faster Margin Expansion and Deleveraging:** The market is likely giving more credit to the "Productivity" leg of the IPP strategy, projecting a much quicker and more substantial increase in the Net Income Margin (e.g., reaching 15% Net Income margin faster than my 12% by 2030). The rapid debt paydown is a tailwind for Net Income, and the market may be projecting a lower interest expense than my conservative estimate.
3.  **Significant Innovation Upside:** The market is likely assigning a high probability and greater revenue contribution from the late-stage blockbuster products, primarily the anticipated launches of new parasiticides and therapeutics in the Pet Health segment. My 5.0% annual growth assumption for 2026-2029 may be too low if these blockbusters materialize on the high end of management's projections.

### **Justification for Conservative Valuation**

My valuation is lower because it adheres to the prompt's mandate for **conservative** assumptions:

*   **Conservative Growth Rate:** I used 5.0% growth in the mid-years, which is at the low-end of "mid-single-digit" (4-6%) organic constant currency growth (which is before FX headwinds and other adjustments) guided by management. I did not bake in high-end expectations for new product launches.
*   **Conservative Margin Ramp-up:** The net income margin expansion from 7.6% (FY 2024) to 12.0% by 2030 is conservative for a pharma company that should gain scale and debt relief.
*   **Very Conservative ROIC (Additional Income):** The 3.0% ROIC used for additional income is a deliberate low-end estimate, reflecting the potential for capital expenditures and the priority of debt paydown over immediate high-yield internal reinvestment.
*   **High Discount Rate:** The 8.0% discount rate is appropriate given the current high-interest-rate environment and ELAN's high debt load, which increases the required return for equity investors.

In summary, the market is assuming high probability for the successful, high-end execution of the IPP strategy, particularly the blockbuster product launches and aggressive margin expansion, while my calculation is based on a conservative, base-case scenario for execution.
