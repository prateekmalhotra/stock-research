1.  **GMV Growth:** Driven by a focus on "enthusiast buyers" and **high-value categories** (Motors, Collectibles, Luxury, Refurbished). Management reported that these focus categories are growing at 10-15%+.
2.  **Take Rate Expansion:** Driven by the full rollout of **Managed Payments** and significant growth in **Advertising revenue** (which is a high-margin revenue stream).

### 3. Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) Valuation

#### A. DCF Assumptions

*   **Discount Rate (Conservative):** **9.0%**
    *   *Justification:* This is a conservative WACC estimate, higher than the latest reported WACC of 9.72% to reflect potential market volatility and competitive pressures in the e-commerce sector.
*   **Terminal Value (Maturity) Rate (Conservative):** **2.0%**
    *   *Justification:* This rate assumes that eBay will continue to grow slightly faster than inflation into perpetuity. It is a conservative assumption, reflecting its mature but profitable business model.

#### B. Net Present Value (NPV) Calculation

The Net Income stream is discounted back to the present day (End of 2025 is treated as Year 1).

#### C. Terminal Value

The Terminal Value is calculated using the Gordon Growth Model:
Terminal Value (TV) = (2030 Net Income * (1 + Maturity Rate)) / (Discount Rate - Maturity Rate)
TV = ($3,885.9 * (1 + 0.02)) / (0.09 - 0.02) = $3,963.6 / 0.07 = $56,623.5 Million

**Terminal Value Net Present Value (TV NPV):**
TV NPV = TV / (1 + Discount Rate)^(Terminal Year)
TV NPV = $56,623.5 / (1.09)^5 = $56,623.5 / 1.539 = **$36,786.5 Million**

#### D. Total NPV

Total NPV = Sum of Projected Cash Flows + TV NPV
Total NPV = $13,531.9M + $36,786.5M = **$50,318.4 Million**

### 5. Conclusion and Justification

**Fair Value Per Share: $110.05**

**Current Stock Price: $79.41**

The calculated fair value of **$110.05 per share** suggests that eBay stock is currently undervalued by approximately $30.64 per share, or about $27.9\%$ of its current market price.

#### Justification for Discrepancy (Valuation vs. Market)

My conservative valuation suggests a price significantly higher than the current market price of $79.41. The market is likely making different, more conservative assumptions than mine:

1.  **Lower GMV Growth:** The market may be discounting the long-term success of the "Focus Category" strategy. While focus categories are growing at 10-15%, the overall marketplace has a substantial portion of declining or flat core GMV. The market may assume lower long-term GMV growth, perhaps closer to 1.5% to 2.0% annually, believing the 'enthusiast' strategy will struggle to offset a general e-commerce deceleration.
2.  **Take Rate Saturation:** My model relies heavily on a successful, continuous expansion of the Take Rate (from 14.03% to 15.70%). The market may assume that the benefits from Managed Payments and Advertising will level off sooner, perhaps stabilizing the Take Rate in the 14.5% to 15.0% range, due to increased seller resistance or intense competition.
3.  **Higher Discount Rate / Lower ROIC:** The market may be assigning a higher risk premium (Discount Rate/WACC) to eBay, given its intense competition from Amazon, Etsy, and specialty marketplaces. A slightly higher Discount Rate (e.g., 10.5% instead of 9.0%) and/or a lower ROIC on reinvested cash (e.g., 8% instead of 12.0%) would significantly reduce the Net Present Value.

**The Case for My Assumption:**

My valuation's higher price is justified by the strong, durable nature of the two key business drivers:
*   **Durable Margin Expansion:** Advertising revenue is a high-margin business, and management's focus on scaling it (currently 2.5% of GMV) is a clear, long-term driver of Net Income Margin expansion, supporting the increase from 21.0% to 22.0% in the model.
*   **Value-Focused GMV Growth:** The focus on high-value, enthusiast categories (like collectibles and motors) insulates eBay from the commoditized bulk-e-commerce competition. The reported 10-15%+ growth in these segments suggests that the overall 3-4% GMV projection for the whole platform is actually **conservative** if management successfully executes its strategy.
*   **Strong Capital Efficiency:** The historical ROIC is strong at $\sim 14.0\%$. The conservative 12.0% ROIC used in the model is very reasonable, showing the company's ability to generate high returns on its internally generated cash, which is a sign of a high-quality, cash-generative business.

The market is likely over-discounting the strategic pivot toward enthusiast buyers and the resulting margin expansion from a higher-mix of advertising revenue, viewing eBay as a legacy e-commerce platform rather than a specialized marketplace with a growing high-margin service business.
