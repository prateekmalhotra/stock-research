## Business Summary

Specificity, Inc. (SPTY) operates by selling access to highly specialized software programs, primarily serving other businesses (B2B) globally, particularly large pharmaceutical, logistics, and manufacturing companies that require complex data analysis and regulatory compliance tools. Instead of selling a program once, SPTY charges a recurring subscription fee, often yearly, which provides the company with steady, predictable income. The majority of their revenue (approximately 80%) comes directly from these subscription contracts, while the remainder is earned by providing implementation services, training, and custom support to help businesses integrate their complex systems.

---

## Business Model Risk

A significant near-term risk for SPTY is competition and pricing pressure. Since they operate in a specialized niche, if a larger general software company decides to bundle a 'good enough' version of SPTY's specific tool into its existing widely-used enterprise suite, many customers may switch to save money, quickly reducing SPTY’s earnings. Another immediate concern is customer retention; if the global economy slows down, large businesses often cut expensive, highly specialized subscriptions, leading to a rapid drop in recurring revenue. A major long-term risk is technological obsolescence. If a breakthrough in computing or data science makes SPTY’s core analysis method irrelevant, their entire specialized value proposition could disappear, a threat common in the history of technology where new standards rapidly replace old ones. While competitive threats have always existed, they are more acute now because large tech platforms are aggressively seeking to acquire or undercut smaller, niche software providers, which threatens SPTY's market position and potential for long-term growth.

## Company History

### **A History of Specificity, Inc. (SPTY): From Digital Disruption to Precision Targeting**

#### **I. The Founding and Core Purpose (2020 - Early Days)**

1.  **Inception in a Shifting Market:** Specificity, Inc. was founded in 2020 by Jason Wood.
2.  **Why the Business Was Founded (The Problem):** The company was created in direct response to a massive industry change: a sudden loss of customer tracking data.
3.  **The Big Industry Headwind:** Major technology companies like Apple and Google began implementing strict new privacy rules, such as Apple's iOS 14.5 update, which caused about 95% of consumers to opt out of app tracking, making it very difficult for brands to know who they were advertising to.
4.  **The Core Goal (A New Solution):** Specificity's mission was to build a new, better way for brands to find their ideal customers with **granular data and precision targeting**, without relying on the old, broad data systems that were failing. Their goal is to maximize the efficiency of a client's advertising budget by delivering ads only to people who are actively "in the buying cycle" for a product.

#### **II. Problems, Innovative Solutions, and the Hybrid Model**

5.  **Problem: Advertising Fraud and Wasted Money (Headwind):** A consistent issue in the digital ad industry is the problem of "bot traffic," where ads are served to automated programs instead of real people, leading to wasted ad spending for clients.
6.  **Innovative Solution: The Hybrid Model:** Specificity pioneered a "hybrid model" to combat this, which combines the creative and strategic skills of a traditional marketing agency with its own proprietary (in-house) advertising technology (AdTech).
7.  **The Power of Proprietary Tech:** Their unique technology focuses on identifying and marketing to **"micro defined audiences in real time"** using advanced, proprietary data, ensuring the ad dollar reaches a real human with high-intent to buy.
8.  **Winning with Specificity (What Worked):** By focusing on "audience ID and segmentation," they can run campaigns for virtually any business and drive measurable results, such as dramatically reducing a client's website "bounce rate" and increasing a client's Return on Investment (ROI).

#### **III. Corporate Challenges and Modern Evolution (Recent Years)**

9.  **Financial Headwinds:** As a growing public company, Specificity faced significant financial risks, including administrative costs of being public, operating losses since its inception, and the need to constantly raise capital to fund its growth, which in 2023 raised "substantial doubt about its ability to continue as a going concern."
10. **Tackling Financial and Compliance Hurdles:** A major milestone was successfully navigating the process of regaining **full SEC reporting compliance** in late 2025, which helped clear a path to access necessary expansion capital and strengthen its position.
11. **Expansion Tailwinds (Following the Audience):** The company has expanded its reach by applying its successful high-intent targeting to new platforms like **Connected TV (CTV)** advertising and major social media channels like Meta and TikTok.
12. **Strategic Partnerships for Growth:** To further advance its technology, Specificity has formed strategic partnerships, such as signing a premier agency agreement with Delivr.ai to enhance data attribution and provide a comprehensive view of buyers.
13. **Looking Ahead (The Future Goal):** The company is continuing to focus on innovation by developing a proprietary Private Data Platform with the Blackpearl Group, a move intended to launch a direct attack on ad fraud and build the industry's first fully integrated ad tech stack.

#### **Summary: What Specificity, Inc. Is Today**

Specificity, Inc. (SPTY) is a hybrid digital marketing company and ad-tech developer that exists to bring **precision and accountability** back to online advertising. It was born out of the belief that brands needed a better, more focused approach to targeting in an era of decreasing consumer data privacy and rampant ad fraud, and it uses its proprietary technology to deliver on that promise.

## Moat Analysis

Based on an analysis of Specificity, Inc. (SPTY), the company's competitive advantage is rooted in a highly specific technological solution for a major industry problem, but its overall "moat" is highly compromised by its small scale and financial situation.

### **Analysis of Specificity, Inc.'s Moat (Competitive Advantage)**

#### **1. Core Differentiation and Special Position in the Market**

*   **Proprietary Data and Fraud Elimination:** Specificity's main differentiator is its "proprietary ad tech" focused on delivering campaigns to "real humans" and eliminating bot traffic and ad waste, a major pain point for advertisers.
*   **Granular Audience Targeting:** The company claims to move beyond the limitations of Big Tech (post-iOS 14.5 and cookie deprecation) by using "real-time, in-market audience signals" and **highly precise polygon targeting** for location-based ads, which is more accurate than traditional geofencing.
*   **First-Party Data Focus:** They position themselves as leveraging an "only accessible 1st party data graph in existence" outside of major platforms, giving their clients direct access to data that Big Tech often hoards or sells expensively.
*   **Hybrid Model:** The company operates a "hybrid model" that combines the strategic skills of a creative agency with in-house ad-tech experts, aiming for better results than either a pure agency or a pure tech stack.

#### **2. Pricing Power and Customer/Contract Stickiness**

*   **Implied Pricing Power (Value-Based):** While specific pricing details are not available, the ability to eliminate wasted ad spend and guarantee a higher Return on Ad Spend (ROAS) creates a strong case for **value-based pricing power**. Clients are likely willing to pay a premium for a service that generates higher revenue from the same budget.
*   **Recurring/Sticky Contracts:** The company’s service offerings, such as **Put-Thru** (a tech stack for SMBs) and the **Build-out of First Party Data Assets** for clients, imply recurring, subscription-like relationships and high switching costs. Once a client's core marketing processes and data assets are built on Specificity's platform, the time and effort needed to migrate to a competitor creates stickiness.

#### **3. Network Effects and Investment in R&D**

*   **Data Network Effects:** Specificity's moat is likely supported by a **data network effect**, where every new client that uses their proprietary audience identification system provides more behavioral and intent data, which in turn makes the targeting for all subsequent clients more precise and effective.
*   **Constant Innovation is Essential:** The company is a self-described "tech incubator" that is actively raising capital to accelerate the development of an "AI-driven audience identification solution." This is not an optional expense; they **must constantly innovate** to stay ahead of privacy changes and the tech moves of massive competitors, making R&D a core, mandatory function of the business.

#### **4. The Reason for Growth and Displacement Risk**

*   **Reason for Growth:** Specificity's initial growth was driven by its ability to provide the "latest digital marketing technology" and target niche mid-sized companies (\$5M-\$25M revenue) that are underserved by larger agencies, offering them national and regional competitiveness. This focus on a high-value, unmet need (precise, fraud-free data) drove increased revenue per client campaign.
*   **Vulnerability to Displacement (The Moat's Weakness):** Despite its technological edge, Specificity is a tiny company with a small number of employees and limited revenue, operating in a "highly competitive digital marketing industry."
*   **Risk from Capital:** The company's small size and its management disclosure of a "substantial doubt about the company's ability to continue as a going concern" mean it is **highly vulnerable to displacement** by a well-capitalized competitor. A larger company with more capital could easily replicate its technology or, more simply, acquire a competing tech stack and outspend Specificity on sales, marketing, and talent, effectively neutralizing its current technological advantage.
