## II. Business Engine Analysis & Revenue Projection

FTAI Aviation operates in two core segments: **Aviation Leasing** and **Aerospace Products**. The valuation model relies heavily on management's forward guidance and the key drivers of the two segments.

### A. Business Engine

1.  **Aerospace Products Segment (The Growth Engine):**
    *   **Driver:** Aftermarket Maintenance, Repair, and Overhaul (MRO) for aging narrowbody aircraft engines, specifically CFM56 and V2500. The market is expanding as airlines extend the useful life of these aircraft (average retirement age has increased from 25 to 30 years).
    *   **Metric:** The company's goal is to reach 25% long-term market share for engine shop visits, up from ~9% annualized in Q2 2025. This high-growth is the primary value driver.
    *   **Margins:** Management has consistently demonstrated a high and stable **34% Adjusted EBITDA margin** in this segment (Q4 2024 and Q2 2025).

2.  **Aviation Leasing Segment (The Asset-Light Pivot):**
    *   **Driver:** The company is pivoting to an "asset-light business model" through the Strategic Capital Initiative (SCI). This means they are monetizing existing assets (asset sales) and partnering with capital providers (like the SCI partnership, which targets deploying $4B in 2025) to generate fee and management income, rather than relying on capital-intensive balance sheet growth.
    *   **Metric:** Management has guided the segment's EBITDA to remain stable at **$500 million** for 2025. This reflects the pivotâ€”trading asset-intensive growth for capital-light stability.

## III. Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) Valuation

### A. Rate Assumptions

*   **Conservative Maturity/Perpetual Growth Rate (g):** Given the strong long-term structural tailwinds (aging narrowbody fleets, MRO shortage) and the projected high growth until 2030, a standard long-term growth rate is justified. I will use a conservative **3.0%**.
*   **Conservative Discount Rate (r):** Given the company's high growth potential, asset-heavy history, and use of debt (high D/E), a higher discount rate is appropriate to reflect the risk and capital structure. I will use a conservative **10.0%**.

| **Sum of NPV (2025 - 2030)** | | | **6,248** |

### C. Terminal Value Calculation

The terminal value represents the value of all cash flows after 2030.

$$
\text{Terminal Value}_{2030} = \frac{\text{Cash Flow}_{2030} \times (1 + \text{g})}{\text{r} - \text{g}}
$$

$$
\text{Terminal Value}_{2030} = \frac{\$2,369 \times (1 + 0.03)}{0.10 - 0.03} = \frac{\$2,440}{0.07} = \$34,857 \text{ million}
$$

**NPV of Terminal Value**

$$
\text{NPV of Terminal Value} = \text{Terminal Value}_{2030} \times \text{Discount Factor}_{t=6}
$$

$$
\text{NPV of Terminal Value} = \$34,857 \times 0.564 = \mathbf{\$19,650 \text{ million}}
$$

## IV. Fair Value Conclusion and Justification

### Fair Value of FTAI Stock: **$221.88 per share**

**Market Value (as of Oct 24, 2025):** **$181.96 per share**

### Justification and Market Assumptions

My calculated Fair Value of **$221.88** is approximately **22% higher** than the current market price of **$181.96**. This significant difference suggests the market is making more conservative assumptions about the company's future.

| My Assumption | Market's Implicit Assumption (Justification for the Gap) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Aerospace EBITDA Growth (2027-2030):** High-growth projection of **30% (2027) down to 15% (2030)**, leading to $1.93B EBITDA in 2030. | **Lower/Slower Growth:** The market may be skeptical of FTAI's ability to quickly scale its market share from 9% to 25% by 2030. A lower growth rate (e.g., 20% down to 10%) would yield a much lower Terminal Value. |
| **Net Income Margin:** A rising and high margin of **20.0% to 22.0%** | **Higher Operating/Financing Costs:** The market may be pricing in higher costs for the massive scale-up required (e.g., labor, MRO facility costs), lower success in the SCI pivot, or simply higher interest rates on their debt. |
| **ROIC:** A robust **10.0%** on reinvested Net Income. | **Lower Capital Efficiency:** The market may view FTAI's asset-light model as less effective at generating a high return on retained earnings, possibly due to low management fee margins or smaller returns on minority investments in the SCI. |
| **Discount Rate (r):** **10.0%** | **Higher Risk Premium:** The market's lower price implies a higher discount rate (closer to **11.5% - 12.0%**) is being used, reflecting greater perceived risk in the business model's execution, capital structure (high D/E), or cyclicality of the aviation industry. |

**Conclusion on Discrepancy:**

The valuation is most sensitive to the growth rate and the discount rate. My model's higher value is primarily due to:
1.  **Confidence in Management's Vision:** I have taken management's forward-looking statements regarding the **$1.4B EBITDA by 2026** and the **25% long-term market share goal** for the high-margin Aerospace segment as the "truth" for the business engine, projecting strong, sustained growth.
2.  **Robust Terminal Value:** The continued high growth rate (15%) into perpetuity and the high profitability assumption create a very large terminal value, which is highly sensitive to the discount rate.

The market is likely applying a higher risk premium and a lower long-term growth expectation, suggesting a **"show-me" story** where the market requires continuous evidence that FTAI can deliver on its high-growth, asset-light MRO strategy before fully pricing it in. If FTAI continues to hit its **$1.4B EBITDA target in 2026** and shows margin stability, the stock price should converge toward the calculated fair value of **$221.88**.
