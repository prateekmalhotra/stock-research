## 4. Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) Calculation

### **Assumptions**

*   **Discount Rate (Conservative but Reasonable):** **10.0%**. Given the high historical ROIC, a lower-risk profile, and the strong balance sheet (low debt), a 10.0% discount rate is reasonable.
*   **Maturity Rate (Conservative Perpetual Growth Rate):** **2.0%**. This is a conservative assumption, reflecting long-term US GDP/inflation.

### **Terminal Value (TV) Calculation**

Terminal Value (TV) reflects the value of all cash flows beyond the projection period (2030).
*   **Free Cash Flow in 2030 (FCF2030):** $1.886B + $0.363B = $2.249 Billion
*   **Terminal Value (2030):** FCF2030 * (1 + Maturity Rate) / (Discount Rate - Maturity Rate)
*   TV = $2.249 Billion * (1 + 0.02) / (0.10 - 0.02)
*   TV = $2.294B / 0.08 = **$28.675 Billion**
*   **Present Value of Terminal Value:** $28.675B * 0.564 (Discount Factor for 2030) = **$16.178 Billion**

## 5. Fair Value Calculation

**Fair Value of Equity** = Total Enterprise Value + Total Cash & Equivalents - Total Debt

*   Fair Value of Equity = $24.599B + $1.971B - $0.900B
*   **Fair Value of Equity** = **$25.670 Billion**

**Fair Value Per Share** = Fair Value of Equity / Shares Outstanding

*   Fair Value Per Share = $25,670,000,000 / 2,923,831
*   **Fair Value Per Share** = **$8,779.67**

### **Justification for Discrepancy (Model vs. Market)**

My calculated Fair Value of **$8,779.67** is **24.15% higher** than the Current Market Price of $7,072.32.

The market is making a more conservative assumption about the future cash flows of NVR than my already conservative model. The primary reasons for this divergence are likely:

1.  **Macroeconomic and Cyclical Risk:** The market is deeply discounting future cash flows due to the high cyclicality of the homebuilding industry. The current macro environment of high interest rates, leading to significant drops in new orders (-16% in Q3 2025) and backlog (-17% in Q3 2025) [cite: 1 in step 2, 3 in step 2], causes investors to fear a prolonged downturn or a complete halt in growth, forcing them to apply a much higher discount rate or a lower growth assumption than my **10.0% discount rate** and **2.0% maturity rate**.
2.  **Margin Pressure Concern:** The market is concerned that the recent drop in Gross Margin to 21.0% [cite: 1 in step 2, 3 in step 2] is not temporary. My model conservatively projects a slow recovery to 16.0% NIM by 2029. The market may be projecting a lower long-term NIM, perhaps 13.0%-14.0%, or a much slower recovery, especially since management guided for "continued margin pressure" [cite: 6 in step 1].
3.  **ROIC Underestimation:** NVR's historical competitive advantage is its phenomenal ROIC (31%-43% historically) [cite: 1 in step 3, 5 in step 3] stemming from its asset-light model. My model uses an extremely conservative **20.0%** ROIC to reflect its high-quality nature while still being conservative for a valuation. The market may not fully believe this high ROIC is sustainable over the full cycle, thus pricing the stock for a much lower reinvestment return.
